\section{算法习题整理 (4.33 $P_{213}$)}
{\bf Problem:} Give lower bound based on decision tree for the number of comparisons needed to merge two sorted segments of length $k$ and $m$, where
$k+m=n, k \leq m$.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Derive an expression that may involves sums, but is exact. \\

    {\bf Solution:} 习题 4.25 已给出merge sort所能形成的排列数,即 $n \choose m$. 所以decision tree的叶子数为 $n \choose m$, lower bound (denoted LB) of number of
    comparisons (height of decision tree) is
    \[
      LB = \log{n \choose m} = \log{\frac{n!}{m! (n-m)!}} = \log{\frac{n!}{m! k!}} = \log{n!} - \log{m!} - \log{k!}.
    \]
    你可以进一步将 $\log{n!}$ 改写成 $\sum$ 的形式.
  \item For $k=m=\frac{n}{2}$, find an approximation in closed form that is close to, but guaranteed to be less than, your expression from part (a).  \\

    {\bf Solution:}  使用 Stirling 公式近似得,
    \[
      \log{n!} \sim n \log{n}, \log{(\frac{n}{2})! \sim \frac{n}{2} \log{\frac{n}{2}}}
    \]
    故 (when $n$ is sufficiently large),
    \[
      LB = n \log{n} - 2 \frac{n}{2}(\log{n} - 1) = n
    \]
    Notice that in Theorem 4.4 ($P_{173}$), the result is $(n-1)$.
  \item Find an approximation in closed from for the case when $k<m$. To get good results, you might wish to consider several ranges for the relationship between $k$ and $n$.  \\

    {\bf Solution:} 使用 Stirling 公式:
    \[
      n! \sim (\frac{n}{e}) ^ {n} \sqrt{2 \pi n}
    \]
    得,
    \[
      LB = n \log{n} - m \log{m} - k \log{k} + \frac{1}{2} \log{\frac{n}{2 \pi mk}}
    \]
    设
    \[
      k = n \alpha, m = n - k = n(1 - \alpha)
    \]
    则有
    \[
      LB = n \big( (\alpha-1) \log{(1-\alpha)} - \alpha \log{\alpha} \big) + \frac{1}{2} \log{\frac{1}{2 \pi n \alpha (1-\alpha)}}
    \]
    对 $\alpha$ 求导, 得
    \[
      (LB)' = n \big( \log{(1-\alpha)} - \log{\alpha} \big) + \frac{2 \alpha - 1}{2 \alpha (1-\alpha) \ln{2}}
    \]
\end{enumerate}
